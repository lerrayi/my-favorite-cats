I thrive on the predictable.

Today, we're supposed to see my favorite band.

I've listened to their whole discography, even the soundtracks they did for movies. I watched all their concerts from the past and tried to guess what they would play today.

We have to take the metro. I've never done it before, but I think things will be okay.

I don't understand the routes. I'm reassured we are going the right way. I'm scared.

Watching the scene pass through the window, I start to relax. It's new, but pretty.

We have to change seats. The train starts and I almost trip. A man urges me to sit next to him.

I don't know what's going to happen. Everything shuts down. My eyes don't know what I'm seeing, and they're welling up with tears.

I spend the rest of the day crying on what was supposed to be one of the happiest days of the year.

I always hated that I was so rigid.
